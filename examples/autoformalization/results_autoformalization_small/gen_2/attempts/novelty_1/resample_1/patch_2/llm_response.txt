<DESCRIPTION>
Key parameters in the original approach were shallow search depth, unbalanced lemma weighting, small beam width, and no syntactic pruning. The new algorithm increases search depth, uses an adaptive lemma-weight schedule that prioritizes commutativity early, increases candidate generation with aggressive syntactic pruning (only terms built from a,b,*,⁻¹), and switches from breadth-first to best-first after a shallow depth. Timeouts and per-candidate limits prevent explosion. These changes produce shorter, more targeted proof searches for the subgroup-abelianness goal while controlling computational cost.
</DESCRIPTION>
